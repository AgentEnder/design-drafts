Brand guidelines · v1 · the product identity for Turnbuckle promotion management

Logo

One mark, one monogram, one idea.

The pictorial mark is a ring corner seen from inside the ring — crimson turnbuckle pads facing you, ropes receding to the left and right, the corner post rising behind. It is drawn at three levels of detail, each tuned to a size band. Never stretch one tier to do another's job.

Behind it sits the same idea reduced to a letter: a turnbuckle is a pad on a post, which is also the shape of a “T”. So the monogram isn't a second concept bolted on — it's the mark distilled to where the pictorial version can't go (favicons, app tiles, anything caption-less and small).

The mark reads most clearly alongside the wordmark, where the name carries the meaning — so the lockup is our primary signature and the bare mark a secondary one. Standalone, with no word to anchor it (favicon, app-icon tile), the silhouette does more work and the read is looser; that's a known tradeoff of an abstract mark, not a defect to caption away.

Full detail: soft underfoot mat, three ropes per side, full post. The mat is a hero-only flourish; it disappears below ~32px and can't carry the mark at small sizes — that's the glyph's job, so drop the mat the moment you drop below the hero band.

Reduced glyph — ≤ 32px (favicon, dense UI)

No mat, one bold rope per pad (all three), a heavier post and fatter strokes — so it stays crisp where anti-aliasing eats thin parallel lines. At 16px this is a silhouette, not a detailed drawing: expect the three pads to merge into a single red bar. That's its floor — at favicon and app-icon sizes the monogram tile below reads far cleaner, so prefer it there.

Monogram — the “T” (favicon, app icon, avatar)

The crossbar is a crimson pad; the stem is the post. It carries the brand where the pictorial mark can't: at 16px it stays a clean, recognisable letter while the corner glyph dissolves.

The dark tile is the default app icon — bright-crimson pad on charcoal-deep reads at every size and pops on a home screen. Use the bare monograms when you need the letter without a tile (inline favicon, a chip, a stamped corner). A solid-crimson tile is intentionally excluded — the pad disappears against a crimson field.

Scaling behaviour

Watch the last four: the corner glyph thins out toward 16px while the monogram tile holds. The handoff from pictorial mark to monogram happens around 24px.

Clear space & minimum size

Keep clear space of at least one pad-height (the gap between two ropes) on all sides — shown by the dashed box. Minimum sizes: lockup ≥ 120px wide · hero mark ≥ 24px · glyph down to 16px · monogram tile ≥ 16px and the only mark cleared for app-icon and favicon use.

Do & don't

✓ DO

  • Use the tier that matches the size band.
  • Use the dark variants on dark surfaces — charcoal dies on dark.
  • Keep crimson pads as the focal color.
  • Give the mark room to breathe.

✗ DON'T

  • Stretch, skew, or rotate the mark.
  • Recolor the pads to a non-brand hue.
  • Shrink the hero mark below 24px — use the glyph, or the monogram tile for favicons.
  • Force the pictorial corner mark into a favicon or app icon — that's the monogram's job.
  • Put the light mark on a busy or crimson background.

Color

Crimson heat, charcoal structure.

This is the product palette — Turnbuckle's own identity. It is deliberately separate from the per-promotion theming customers configure (their own primary/secondary/accent). Product crimson should never be presented as a tenant's brand color.

Crimson and the design system's danger red sit close on the wheel, so keep them apart in function, not just in hex: crimson never marks a destructive action (delete, cancel, remove), and a crimson CTA should never sit shoulder-to-shoulder with a danger button competing for the same red. When the two would collide, danger keeps the red and the brand action steps back to charcoal. If you can't tell at a glance which red means "go" and which means "gone," the layout is wrong.

Typography

Inter, set tight.

Inter is the product typeface — it is already the app's default font, so brand and UI stay consistent. The wordmark is Inter ExtraBold (800) with -1 tracking. For shipped logo files the wordmark should be converted to outlines so it renders identically without the font installed.

Voice & Tone

Kayfabe out front, spreadsheet in back.

Turnbuckle is run by people who love the business and need to run it well. We talk like a seasoned promoter who is also organized — energetic and fluent in the craft, but never hokey, and always reliable when it counts.

Principles

HOW WE SOUND

  • Talk like you book shows, not like enterprise software. "Add a match," not "Create segment entity."
  • Respect the craft. Use real wrestling vocabulary correctly — card, segment, run-in, over, draw.
  • Be the reliable one. It runs live events; functional copy is plain and unambiguous.
  • Save swagger for the marquee. Landing pages can have heat; in-app microcopy stays calm.

WHAT WE AVOID

  • Forced wrestling puns on every button ("Body-slam your data!").
  • Corporate filler — "leverage," "synergy," "solutions."
  • Cutesy errors when something real broke.
  • Breaking kayfabe cynically — we're fans, not above it.
