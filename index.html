Logo

One mark, three tiers.

The mark is a ring corner seen from inside the ring — crimson turnbuckle pads facing you, ropes receding to the left and right, the corner post rising behind. It is drawn at three levels of detail, each tuned to a size band. Never stretch one tier to do another's job.

The mark reads most clearly alongside the wordmark, where the name carries the meaning — so the lockup is our primary signature and the bare mark a secondary one. We don't ask the mark to carry the brand by itself in a caption-less slot, because it almost never appears that way: a favicon sits beside its tab title, an app icon beside its label. So we shrink this mark into those slots rather than inventing a separate glyph to decode in isolation.

Full detail: soft underfoot mat, three ropes per side, full post. The mat is a hero-only flourish; it disappears below ~32px and can't carry the mark at small sizes — that's the glyph's job, so drop the mat the moment you drop below the hero band.

Reduced glyph — ≤ 32px (favicon, dense UI)

icons/glyph/vector-dark.svg · on dark

No mat, one bold rope per pad (all three), a heavier post and fatter strokes — so it stays crisp where anti-aliasing eats thin parallel lines. At 16px this is a silhouette, not a detailed drawing: the three pads merge into a single red bar and the ropes fade. That's fine — at favicon size this same glyph goes on a tile (below), and the tab title beside it carries the recognition.

Favicon & app icon

The browser makes you ship a favicon, and the bare glyph turns to mush at 16px — so the glyph goes on a tile. This isn't a new mark; it's the same corner mark, reduced and seated on charcoal so it stays a crisp, on-brand square. The dark tile is the default.

At 16px (pinned/overflowing tabs) the ropes fade and you're left with a dark square holding a red core — soft, but never seen alone: the tab title or app label is always adjacent and does the recognising. We don't optimise this slot for standalone legibility because nothing here is standalone. Rasterised PNG/ICO set lives in icons/tile/; icons/tile/vector-square.svg is the full-bleed source for OS-masked icons.

Scaling behaviour

Below ~32px the bare glyph thins out; on the tile it holds its shape as a square stamp. Use the bare glyph in dense UI where there's already a surface behind it, and the tile wherever the OS wants a square (favicon, app icon).

Clear space & minimum size

Keep clear space of at least one pad-height (the gap between two ropes) on all sides — shown by the dashed box. Minimum sizes: lockup ≥ 120px wide · hero mark ≥ 24px · glyph down to 16px · favicon/app-icon tile down to 16px.

Do & don't

✓ DO

  • Use the tier that matches the size band.
  • Use the dark variants on dark surfaces — charcoal dies on dark.
  • Keep crimson pads as the focal color.
  • Give the mark room to breathe.

✗ DON'T

  • Stretch, skew, or rotate the mark.
  • Recolor the pads to a non-brand hue.
  • Shrink the hero mark below 24px — use the glyph, or the tile for favicons.
  • Use the bare glyph as a favicon — put it on the tile so it stays a clean square.
  • Put the light mark on a busy or crimson background.

Color

Crimson heat, charcoal structure.

This is the product palette — Turnbuckle's own identity. It is deliberately separate from the per-promotion theming customers configure (their own primary/secondary/accent). Product crimson should never be presented as a tenant's brand color.

Crimson and the design system's danger red sit close on the wheel, so keep them apart in function, not just in hex: crimson never marks a destructive action (delete, cancel, remove), and a crimson CTA should never sit shoulder-to-shoulder with a danger button competing for the same red. When the two would collide, danger keeps the red and the brand action steps back to charcoal. If you can't tell at a glance which red means "go" and which means "gone," the layout is wrong.

Typography

Inter, set tight.

Inter is the product typeface — it is already the app's default font, so brand and UI stay consistent. The wordmark is Inter ExtraBold (800) with -1 tracking. For shipped logo files the wordmark should be converted to outlines so it renders identically without the font installed.

Voice & Tone

Kayfabe out front, spreadsheet in back.

Turnbuckle is run by people who love the business and need to run it well. We talk like a seasoned promoter who is also organized — energetic and fluent in the craft, but never hokey, and always reliable when it counts.

Principles

HOW WE SOUND

  • Talk like you book shows, not like enterprise software. "Add a match," not "Create segment entity."
  • Respect the craft. Use real wrestling vocabulary correctly — card, segment, run-in, over, draw.
  • Be the reliable one. It runs live events; functional copy is plain and unambiguous.
  • Save swagger for the marquee. Landing pages can have heat; in-app microcopy stays calm.

WHAT WE AVOID

  • Forced wrestling puns on every button ("Body-slam your data!").
  • Corporate filler — "leverage," "synergy," "solutions."
  • Cutesy errors when something real broke.
  • Breaking kayfabe cynically — we're fans, not above it.
